About Me

I am a fourth year PhD student at UT Austin, advised by Prof. Inderjit Dhillon. I am also a Student Researcher at Google, working closely with Prateek Jain. My research interests lie in Large Scale Machine Learning, with a focus on efficiency and scalability. I aim to build fundamental ML algorithms that exhibit strong empirical performance coupled with real-world deployability. In a quiet affirmation of their utility, a few of these algorithms have been integrated into the workflows of Google and Microsoft, among other organisations.

In a fantastic previous life, I earned my Bachelors from BITS Pilani, Goa. During my stint at Microsoft Research India, I had the fortune of having Ravishankar Krishnaswamy as my undergraduate thesis advisor, and worked closely with Harsha Simhadri.
